South Africa

Becoming an Electrostate

South Africa is poised to become an electrostate — unlocking massive growth by electrifying transport, industry and private life through renewables.

  1. Decarbonize and electrify the power system, replacing coal with clean energy.
  2. Electrify different sectors to substitute fossil molecules with electrons.

CENEC drives this transition by decarbonizing industry in parallel with power sector transformation — creating synergies for green hydrogen, advanced manufacturing and low-carbon exports.

Regional Energy Market

South African Power Pool

The SAPP, linking 12 SADC countries, faces a 4,200 MW power deficit but is rapidly evolving — trading volumes are growing and private players are entering the market.

  • Grid expansion: connecting Malawi, Tanzania & Angola by 2028
  • ReTrade SAPP modernisation, backed by the World Bank
  • Dedicated green power market under development
  • Billions unlocked for cross-border renewable infrastructure
